What HyFlex Is

Flexible Participation

Our HyFlex (Hybrid-Flexible) courses provide flexible participation options for modern adult education students with responsibilities that require alternatives to traditional learning schedules.

Learning Options

These classes are provided by experienced instructors across our partner counties (Clay, Knox, Laurel, and Whitley) with 1.5 hour lessons on math, language arts, science, or social studies topics. Times and days are varied to accommodate the maximum number of students.

How to Enroll

1. Contact your county provider

You can find the contact information for all five of our adult education centers at the bottom of the page, or you can use the form below to send us your information.

2. Schedule orientation and enrollment

Make an appointment to visit one of our learning centers and get to know the program. Book a time to take our low-stakes pre-assessments in reading and math so we can begin to tailor instruction to your needs.

3. Complete paperwork and testing

Fill out the required paperwork and take the pre-tests. This process usually takes around 2.5 hours--then you're done and ready for classes!

About Our Programs

All adult education services are free of charge. We provide classes, tests to help determine what you should study, study materials (online and in paper), and practice testing. The only thing you might ever pay for are the official GED exams once you've passed a practice (Ready) test.

The regular cost of these tests are $30-36 each. However, Kentucky students can often take advantage of statewide promotions that reduce or waive fees altogether for a first attempt at tests, making it free! Test scholarships are also available through donations of those who support our mission.
